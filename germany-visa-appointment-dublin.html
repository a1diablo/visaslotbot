How Germany visa appointments work in Dublin

Unlike most Schengen countries in Ireland that use VFS Global, Germany uses a separate booking platform called VisaMetric (ie-appointment.visametric.com). This is the official appointment system for the German Embassy in Dublin.

The process is relatively straightforward:

1. Go to ie-appointment.visametric.com
2. Select your visa category (Schengen, National, etc.)
3. Fill in the required details and solve the captcha
4. If slots are available, you'll see dates on the calendar to pick from

The good news: you don't need an account or login to check availability. The bad news: step 4 almost never shows any dates. Slots are released without warning and disappear in minutes.

Why VisaMetric slots are so scarce

The German Embassy in Dublin processes Schengen visa applications for all of Ireland. With limited appointment capacity and consistently high demand, the booking calendar is empty more often than not.

VisaMetric doesn't publish a schedule for when new slots will be released. They can appear at any time of day or night, and they're booked almost immediately. If you're manually checking the site a few times a day, the odds of catching a fresh batch of slots are extremely low.

Each check also requires solving a captcha, which adds friction. By the time you've filled in the form and verified you're human, any newly released slots may already be gone.

VisaMetric vs VFS Global

If you've booked visa appointments for other Schengen countries in Dublin (Austria, Netherlands, Switzerland), you used VFS Global. VisaMetric is a different system with different quirks:

No account required. VFS Global requires you to create an account and log in to check availability. VisaMetric lets you check the calendar without any registration, though you still need to solve a captcha each time.

Captcha-gated sessions. Every VisaMetric session starts with a captcha. Sessions expire after about 20 minutes, requiring you to solve another one. VFS Global uses Cloudflare protection instead, which is less visible but equally restrictive for automated access.

API-based availability checks. VisaMetric uses an internal API to fetch available dates, which makes automated monitoring technically different from VFS Global's calendar-based approach. Both systems require specialised tools to monitor reliably.

Same scarcity problem. Whether it's VFS Global or VisaMetric, Dublin Schengen visa slots are scarce across the board. The underlying issue is limited embassy capacity, not the booking platform itself.

Tips for booking a Germany visa appointment

Have your documents ready. When you get an alert, you need to move fast. Have your passport details, travel dates, and supporting documents prepared before you start checking for slots.

Know the form fields. VisaMetric asks for your visa category, personal details, and passport information before showing available dates. Familiarise yourself with the form so you can fill it in quickly when a slot alert arrives.

Enable Telegram notifications. Slots can appear at any time, including early mornings and weekends. Make sure your phone alerts you immediately when a message arrives in the alerts channel.

Book the first available date. Don't scroll through the calendar looking for a more convenient date. Take whatever is available first. You can always try to reschedule later if better dates open up.

Use a fast internet connection. The window between a slot appearing and being booked is measured in minutes. A slow connection or mobile data could cost you the appointment.

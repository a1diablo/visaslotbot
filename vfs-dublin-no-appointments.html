They do. Here's what's actually going on.

Why VFS Dublin shows no appointments

VFS Global Dublin is the only visa application centre in Ireland for several Schengen countries. Everyone in the country needing a visa to Switzerland, Austria, Liechtenstein, or Slovenia books through the same office.

The problem is how slots are released:

  • Unpredictable timing. New slots appear at random times. There's no fixed schedule — it could be 9am on a Monday or 11pm on a Saturday.
  • Tiny batches. VFS might release 3-5 slots at a time, not dozens. With hundreds of people checking, they're gone within minutes.
  • No queue system. It's first-come, first-served. If you're not checking at the exact moment slots appear, you miss them.

So when you see "no appointments available", it doesn't mean slots never open. It means you're checking at the wrong time.

What doesn't work

Refreshing manually throughout the day. You'd need to check every few minutes, 24 hours a day. That's not realistic. Even if you check 20 times a day, you're only covering 20 random moments out of 1,440 minutes.

Checking only during business hours. Slots have appeared in the evening and on weekends. Limiting yourself to 9-5 means missing off-hours releases.

Browser extensions that auto-refresh. VFS uses Cloudflare protection. Automated page refreshes will get your session blocked, and you'll need to re-login through CAPTCHAs.

Keep your VFS account ready. Create your account, verify your email, and have your login details saved. Don't waste time on setup when a slot appears.

Have your documents prepared. Know your visa category, have your passport details handy, and be ready to fill in the booking form quickly.

Enable notifications. If you're using an alert service, make sure Telegram notifications are turned on and your phone isn't on silent. A 30-second delay can mean the difference between getting a slot and missing it.

Be flexible on dates. When a slot opens, take it — even if it's not your ideal date. You can try to reschedule later, but an imperfect date is better than no appointment at all.

Which countries are affected

The Dublin VFS centre handles visa applications for Switzerland, Austria, Liechtenstein, Slovenia, Netherlands, Germany, and Spain. All four countries share the same appointment pool through the Austrian Embassy, so the slot scarcity affects all of them equally.

If you need a Schengen visa from Ireland for any of these countries, you're competing for the same limited slots.

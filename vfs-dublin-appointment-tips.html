How to Get a Visa Appointment in Dublin: Practical Tips That Actually Work

You have checked VFS Global at 7am, 12pm, and 11pm. You have refreshed the page dozens of times. You have Googled "how to get VFS appointment Dublin" and found nothing but generic advice. The calendar is always empty, and you are running out of time before your trip.

Most of the advice online boils down to "keep trying." That is not a strategy. Here is what actually works.

Why appointment slots are so hard to get

Dublin handles Schengen visa applications for all of Ireland. Whether you are applying through VFS Global (Austria, Switzerland, Netherlands), Prenotami (Italy), VisaMetric (Germany), or BLS (Spain), the core problem is the same.

Tiny supply, massive demand. Each embassy releases a limited number of slots per week. With thousands of applicants competing for the same handful of appointments, slots disappear within minutes of being released.

Unpredictable release times. New slots do not appear on a fixed schedule. They can show up at 9am on a Tuesday or 3pm on a Saturday. There is no pattern to reverse-engineer.

No advance notice. None of these booking systems announce when slots will open. You either happen to be checking at the right moment, or you miss them entirely.

What doesn't work

Manual refreshing. Checking the booking page a few times a day gives you almost zero chance of catching a slot. The window between slots appearing and being booked is often under five minutes.

Browser auto-refresh extensions. VFS Global uses Cloudflare protection. Automated page refreshes from browser extensions get detected and blocked. Even if they work initially, your session will be flagged and you will hit a CAPTCHA wall.

Paying agents or "consultants." Some agencies charge hundreds of euros to book an appointment for you. They have no special access to the system. They are either refreshing manually (same odds as you) or using bots that violate terms of service. Either way, it is expensive with no guarantee.

Checking only during business hours. Slots are released at all hours. Limiting your checks to 9-to-5 means you are missing most release windows.

What actually works

The only reliable approach is automated monitoring with instant alerts. Instead of manually checking the booking page, a monitoring system checks every few minutes around the clock. The moment a slot appears, you receive a notification on your phone.

This works because the monitor is always running. It does not sleep, forget, or get distracted. When slots open at 2am on a Sunday, you get an alert. You wake up, open the booking page, and grab the slot before it vanishes.

The key advantage is speed. A human checking manually might notice a slot 30 minutes after it appears. An automated monitor detects it in under two minutes and sends the alert immediately. That difference is usually the gap between getting an appointment and missing it.

Step-by-step preparation checklist

Getting an alert is only half the battle. You need to be ready to act the moment it arrives. Here is how to prepare.

1. Create your booking account now. Do not wait until you receive an alert to register on VFS Global, Prenotami, VisaMetric, or BLS. Create your account today, verify your email, and make sure you can log in without issues. Every minute you spend setting up an account is a minute someone else is booking the last slot.

2. Upload your documents in advance. Some booking systems ask you to attach documents (passport copy, photo, GNIB card, proof of accommodation) during the booking process. Have all of these saved as PDFs on both your phone and computer. Do not scramble to scan documents when a slot is available.

3. Enable Telegram notifications with sound. If you are using an alert service, make sure notifications are not silenced. Go into Telegram settings, find the alerts channel, and set it to always notify with sound. You need to hear this alert even if your phone is in your pocket or on a nightstand.

4. Keep the booking page open in a browser tab. When you get an alert, you want to be one click away from the calendar. Keep a tab open with the booking page loaded. On VFS, stay on the appointment calendar page. On Prenotami, stay logged in and keep the services page ready.

5. Book the first date you see, reschedule later. Do not browse through the calendar looking for the perfect date. Select the first available slot and confirm. Most systems allow you to reschedule once you have a booking. An imperfect date is infinitely better than no appointment at all.

6. Have payment ready. Some centres charge a fee at the time of booking. Have your credit or debit card within reach. Do not lose a slot because you had to go find your wallet.

Country-specific tips

Austria, Switzerland, Liechtenstein, Slovenia (VFS Global). These countries share a single VFS booking portal for Dublin. The calendar uses a Turnstile CAPTCHA, so even checking availability requires human interaction. Slots are released in small batches and go fast. Read our VFS Dublin guide.

Netherlands (VFS Global). The Netherlands uses VFS Global but has a separate booking flow with its own category and subcategory selection. You must pick "Tourist" or the correct visa type before the calendar appears. Read our Netherlands guide.

Germany (VisaMetric). Germany uses VisaMetric instead of VFS. The good news: you do not need to create an account or log in to check availability. The bad news: the system uses a CAPTCHA that must be solved for each session. Read our Germany guide.

Italy (Prenotami). Italy runs its own system called Prenotami, managed by the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs. You must register an account and log in through SSO before you can even see if slots exist. The system either shows a booking form or a rejection message. There is no calendar preview. Read our Italy guide.

Spain (BLS International). Spain uses BLS Global for Dublin appointments. The login process includes a grid-based CAPTCHA, and available dates are embedded in the booking page source. Read our Spain guide.

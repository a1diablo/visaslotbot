How Italy visa appointments work in Dublin

Unlike most Schengen countries that use VFS Global, Italy runs its own booking system called Prenot@mi (prenotami.esteri.it). This is the official appointment platform of the Italian Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

To book an appointment at the Italian Embassy in Dublin, you need to:

1. Register an account on Prenot@mi and select the Dublin embassy
2. Log in through the SSO system at iam.esteri.it
3. Go to "Prenota" (Book) and click on "Visti Schengen e Nazionali"
4. If slots are available, you'll see a calendar to pick your date

The problem is step 4 almost never happens. Slots are released irregularly and booked within minutes.

Why Prenot@mi slots are so scarce

The Italian Embassy in Dublin handles all Schengen visa applications for Ireland. With only a small number of appointment slots per week and growing demand, the booking page is essentially a waiting game.

Prenot@mi doesn't announce when new slots will be released. They appear at unpredictable times and are snapped up almost immediately. Manually refreshing the page throughout the day is exhausting and usually fruitless.

Making it worse, Prenot@mi requires you to be logged in to even check availability. Each check requires navigating through the SSO login, going to the services page, and clicking the booking button. It's slow enough that by the time you complete the process, any newly released slots may already be gone.

Prenot@mi vs VFS Global

If you've used VFS Dublin for other countries (Switzerland, Austria, Netherlands), you'll notice Prenot@mi is quite different:

Login required to check availability. VFS lets you browse the calendar without logging in. Prenot@mi requires SSO authentication before you can even see if slots exist.

No calendar preview. VFS shows a calendar with available dates. Prenot@mi either shows a booking form (slots exist) or a rejection dialog (no slots). There's no way to see upcoming availability.

Different protection systems. VFS uses Cloudflare. Prenot@mi uses Radware Bot Manager. Both are designed to prevent automated access, which makes building a reliable monitor technically challenging.

Tips for booking an Italy visa appointment

Keep your Prenot@mi account ready. Register at prenotami.esteri.it, select the Dublin embassy, and verify your email. Don't wait for an alert to create your account.

Stay logged in. Prenot@mi sessions expire, but you can speed up the booking process by having the site open in a browser tab. When you get an alert, you'll only need to navigate to the booking page.

Enable Telegram notifications. Slots can appear at any time. Make sure your phone alerts you immediately when a message arrives in the alerts channel.

Book instantly. When an alert arrives, don't deliberate. Go to Prenot@mi, click "Prenota" on the Visti Schengen row, and select the first available date. You can always try to reschedule later.

Upload documents in advance. Prenot@mi may ask you to attach documents (passport copy, GNIB card) when booking. Have these ready as PDF files on your phone or computer.
